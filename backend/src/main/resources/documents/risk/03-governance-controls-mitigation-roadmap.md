## Governance Controls and Mitigation Roadmap (Examples)

### Control categories (common enterprise model)
- Prevent: reduce likelihood (access control, secure configuration, patching)
- Detect: improve visibility (logging, monitoring, anomaly detection, alerting)
- Respond: contain and handle incidents (incident response playbooks, escalation)
- Recover: restore operations (backup/restore, disaster recovery drills)

### Governance actions
- Define risk ownership and decision rights (risk owner, control owner, approval path).
- Establish policy baselines and control objectives aligned with recognized standards.
- Ensure evidence collection and audit readiness.
- Set review and improvement cadence (e.g., quarterly risk review).

### Implementation roadmap (practical)
- Phase 0: scope confirmation and evidence gap assessment.
- Phase 1: quick wins (high-impact/low-effort mitigations).
- Phase 2: medium-term control maturity improvements.
- Phase 3: long-term architecture and process enhancements.

### Metrics and monitoring
- Coverage: % assets with control evidence
- Effectiveness: incident reduction, mean time to detect/respond/recover
- Compliance: audit pass rate, policy exception rate
